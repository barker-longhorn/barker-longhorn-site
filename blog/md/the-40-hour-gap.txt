Angel investors who spend over 40 hours on due diligence average 7.1x returns. Those who spend under 20 hours? 1.1x. That's from a study of 1,137 exits across 539 angel investors ([Wiltbank & Boeker, Kauffman Foundation](https://www.angelcapitalassociation.org/data/Documents/Resources/AngelGroupResarch/1d%20-%20Resources%20-%20Research/6%20RSCH_-_ACEF_-_Returns_to_Angel_Investor_in_Groups.pdf)). The pattern is clear: deeper diligence means better returns. The problem is most early-stage investors don't have 40 hours per deal. DOCRA closes that gap.
